<objective>
Implement D-27 (Claude's discretion) — smooth entity transitions on low-end Android TV box hardware. Research recommendation backed by Chromium compositing + Framer Motion performance tier list:

**Chosen strategy (Option 4 + Alternative simplification from research):**

1. Replace horizontal slide (`x: "100%" → 0 → "-100%"`) with opacity fade (`opacity: 0 → 1 → 0`)
2. Reduce transition duration from 0.45s to 0.35s
3. Keep `mode="sync"` for cross-dissolve
4. **Permanently remove `backdrop-blur-md`** from all presentation-mode MetricCard / ArtistCard elements (simpler than conditional removal, per research "Alternative (simpler)" note — visually acceptable at TV viewing distance because `bg-white/[0.03]` on a dark banner already minimizes blur contribution)

Rationale (from research §8):

- Opacity is the S-Tier compositor-thread animation in Chromium — cheapest possible on Android TV
- `backdrop-filter: blur()` triggers a separate compositing layer per element; with 6+ cards visible per entity × 2 entities simultaneously during transition = 12+ blur layers. Removing eliminates the layer explosion permanently.
- Permanent removal > conditional removal: no `isTransitioning` state machine, no `onAnimationStart`/`onAnimationComplete` coordination, no Pitfall 4 (1-frame flash risk)

Depends on 03-05: shares `dashboard-client.tsx`. Running after 03-05 ensures the auto-hide wiring doesn't conflict with the AnimatePresence edit.

Purpose: Eliminate transition stutter on TV box.
Output: Fade transitions in dashboard-client.tsx + backdrop-blur removal across presentation cards.
</objective>
